---
title: "I made a decision"
layout: post
---

Perseverence is hard. Like, really hard. I get up at 5 am, make breakfast for
the kids. Get the kids ready for day care. Drop them off at day care. Get home,
ponder whether to do something for my health, like going for a run, or start
the grid at day job. My work day ends with distant screams, supposedly coming
from kids. My kids. Yes, these are my kids. Messaging my goodbye on Slack to
end my work day, composing myself to perform a sudden switch from engineer to
dad, prepared to defuse yet another drama of the sorts "my sister opened the
door first, but I wanted to open the door first. It's totally unfair!",
finalized by some maniacally screaming. Boy, our stairwell sure has great
acoustics.

After managing expectations and shepharding our kids from dinner to bed, I end
up with around one hour left before I need to go to bed without ending up
sleep-deprived the next day. That's when I'm faced with the challenge of each
day: what do I to in that precious time? I'm so tired, but it's the only time I
have left, so I should use it wisely, shouldn't I?

Plenty of advice online when searching for an answer to this question. Ranging
from "Be kind to yourself and relax if you're tired. There's always tomorrow."
to "Get to work. No distractions! You have to put in the work. Use every
opportunity you can get!"

But, what do *I* want to do? And what does *want* even mean? Because I can
easily want to open YouTube, Netflix, or whatever, and just mindlessly consume
something entertaining enough. It's *so* easy and convenient to pass time like
this.

However, that doesn't make me feel like I'm spending my time wisely. No sense
of purpose for me on that one. Nothing I can be proud of. Nothing that makes me
feel like I accomplished something.

So, I made the decision to drop that habit and put in the work instead. What
work? Open-source contributions, blogging, writing code, building side
projects.
