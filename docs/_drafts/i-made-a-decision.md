---
title: "I made a decision: consume less, create more."
layout: post
---

Perseverence is hard. Like, really hard. I get up at 5 am, make breakfast for
the kids. Get them ready and drop them off at day care. Get home, ponder
whether to do something for my health, like going for a run, or start grinding
at day job early. My work day is coming to an end. I hear distant screams,
supposedly coming from kids. My kids. Yes, these are my kids. I message my
goodbye on Slack to announce the end of my work day and prepare myself to
switch from engineer to dad mode, defusing the situation that is approaching.

After managing expectations and shepharding our kids from dinner to bed, I am
left with roughly one hour left before I need to go to bed without ending up
sleep-deprived the next day. One hour. That's when I'm faced with the challenge
of the day: how do I spend this precious hour?

Most often I mindlessly open YouTube and check my subscriptions. Surely I find
a blue dot next to at least one of my subscribed channels, indicating there is
new content waiting to be consumed. "But, I planned to work on something
creative tonight." I catch myself thinking, with some guilt of self-betrayal
waiting around the corner.
"Just one short video to decompress. Then I'll get to the creative part of the
evening!"

And that's when I buried my creativity for that day.

So, I made the decision to put in the work. No more YouTube. No more Netflix.
Instead of *consuming*, I want to *create*. Whatever it may be: open-source
contributions, blog posts, side projects.
